## 1. Spiritual Briefing — "Know Before You Go" (verified)

France is the heart of Western secularism. Historically Catholic — the "eldest daughter of the
Church" — it is now one of the least religiously practicing nations in Europe. The barrier here is
**not persecution or law** (evangelism is fully legal under *laïcité*); it is **indifference and
intellectual skepticism**. Yet evangelicals are now the majority of French Protestants and a new
evangelical church opens roughly every ten days.

## 5. Cultural Knowings (do's & don'ts)
- ✅ **Always say "Bonjour" first** — before any question or entering a shop. Skipping it is rude.
- ✅ **Use *vous*, not *tu*** with strangers/elders; *tu* only once invited.
- ✅ Greetings: handshake on first/formal meetings; *la bise* (2–4 cheek kisses by region) for friends — don't hug strangers.
- ✅ Dress neatly and understated; avoid athletic wear, loud logos, flip-flops in the city.
- ✅ Expect debate, not hostility — stay warm, not defensive.
- ❌ Don't be loud in public. ❌ Don't go aggressive/tract-first — relational and testimony-based fits the culture.
